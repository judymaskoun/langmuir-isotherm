Derivation of the isotherm

1. Start from equilibrium

rate = dCA/dt = rate adsorption − rate desorption

At equilibrium, the net change in concentration of A is zero.

Therefore, adsorption and desorption rates are equal:

k1CAM(1 − θ) = k−1Mθ

M is the total number of adsorption sites, so it appears on both sides and cancels.

k1CA(1 − θ) = k−1θ
2. Solve for site coverage

Expand the left side:

k1CA − k1CAθ = k−1θ

Move all terms containing θ to one side:

k1CA = θ(k−1 + k1CA)

Then isolate θ:

θ =k1CAk−1 + k1CA
3. Define the adsorption constant

Divide the numerator and denominator by k−1, then define:

Kads =k1k−1

This gives the fractional coverage form:

θ =KadsCA1 + KadsCA
4. Convert coverage to adsorption amount

θ is the fraction of adsorption sites occupied. If qmax is the maximum adsorption capacity, then:

θ = q/qmax

So q = qmaxθ. Substitute the expression for θ:

q =qmaxKadsCA1 + KadsCA
